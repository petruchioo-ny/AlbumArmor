Our Story

For the love of music — protect what you play.

How one collector's frustration became a mission.

🎧 Photo of Piotr Or sketch of early prototypes
How it started

It started with a crack.

I still remember the first crackle — that sharp, sinking sound when a record doesn't make it.

After years of saving and crate-digging, I finally bought my first turntable. Saturdays became sacred: flipping through dusty bins, reading handwritten price tags, talking pressings with anyone who cared to listen. Every record I brought home felt like a story rescued from time.

Then one day, a long-awaited LP arrived in the mail — bent at the corner, warped, unplayable. A few weeks later, it happened again. And again. The music I loved wasn't fading with age — it was being lost to bad packaging.

I didn't get angry. I got curious.

"How could something made to last a lifetime be lost to a box that lasts a day?"

— Piotr, Founder

From Frustration to Invention

Seventeen prototypes later, AlbumArmor® was born.

In my small Brooklyn apartment, surrounded by sleeves, rulers, and scraps of cardboard, I started experimenting. I studied how mailers failed — crushed corners, split seams, too much internal movement. I tested fold lines, compression strength, and edge durability. At night, I sketched new designs. On weekends, I mailed prototypes to friends and record shops, asking them to be brutally honest.

Version 1 buckled. Version 5 was too heavy. Version 12 worked for one record, but two still rattled.

Then came Version 17 — the one that changed everything. Adjustable height folds that hugged anywhere from one to eight LPs, reinforced edges that resisted corner dings, and a quick, tape-free assembly that made packing feel almost as satisfying as dropping the needle on Side A.

That's when AlbumArmor® was born.

📦 Early prototype photo Brooklyn kitchen table, 2023
